Reflection

This portfolio exhibits the work I completed in ENGL-361, Technical Writing. Technical writing is an important skill in all industries, but especially software engineering. In an industry increasingly omnipresent, it's more important than ever to be able to communicate complex ideas to a wide audience.

Below, I have provided several different types of documents:

  • a company-wide Memo, one of the most important forms of communication in business.
  • a detailed Description of a physical object.
  • an Extended Definition brochure, for explaining complicated ideas concisely and simply.
  • a Research Proposal, a long-form and thorough document.
  • a journalistic Article for Rochester Institute of Technology's student-run magazine, Reporter.

I selected these pieces in order to illustrate the way my experience in creative writing and my experience in software engineering combine to allow me to bring a unique perspective to my work. I'm left-brained and right-brained, and I designed this responsive, mobile-ready site to reflect that.

Thanks for checking out my work!

Memo

This memo, written for a homework assignment, is meant to serve as a gentle reminder and directive for the employees of the fictional Agriflex Corporation regarding the company's email policy. How do you ensure that 100% of readers -- who are likely quickly skimming rather than really reading -- come away from the memo with a full understanding of your overall point? This memo was written to answer that question.

Description

This document seeks to give a detailed, thorough description of an Automatic Laser Pointer cat toy. The aim of this description was to ensure that even readers who had never seen such a device would be able to recognize it and operate it with ease.

Extended Definition

Fatigue is a huge issue for the trucking industry. This brochure describes, for the layperson, exactly what fatigue is, how it affects the trucking industry, and how to mitigate it. With a visually interesting design and tons of statistics, this brochure also advertises a fatigue-risk-mitigating product.

Proposal

This proposal thoroughly outlines a fictional research project that investigates algorithmic gender bias in resume ranking websites. For example, do men's resumes tend to be ranked higher than women's resumes? If so, why? Understanding the nuanced ways that algorithms may reinforce harmful stereotypes is a pet obsession of mine.

Journalistic Article

In my time at RIT, I was lucky enough to write for Reporter Magazine, RIT's student-run magazine. This article, "Home for the Holidays," examines cross-cultural winter holiday traditions practiced by RIT students from Austria, Vietnam, South Korea, Costa Rica, and India.
